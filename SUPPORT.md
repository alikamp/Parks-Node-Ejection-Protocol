## About Me

Hurricane Iniki tore through my town in 1992. The island never fully recovered — severe economic slowdown through the late 90s, and Kauai rents have sat in the top 0.5% nationally for the past twenty years.

I joined the Navy in January 2000. Qualified for nuclear engineering, declined in favor of special warfare. Served through Iraq, couldn't reconcile the civilian casualties I was seeing, and left. Received an OTH discharge. Returned home.

2008 hit. Rents kept climbing. I decided I needed to do more.

Started inventing — physical products, hardware prototypes. Some got stolen. Pivoted to tech. Applied to accelerators in every form imaginable. Had a cofounder, built product. Got a phone interview with one accelerator in summer 2019 — me on one end, four of them on the other. When they asked why I wanted to attend, I told them there would be a massive economic shutdown due to a pandemic in March 2020 and my startup would be well positioned for it.

Didn't get in. March came and went exactly as I described. Figured I'd get a call back. 2021 — nothing. 2022 — nothing. Wasn't good enough.

Applied to Y Combinator roughly twenty times over the past two decades. In 2012, in their application section that asks "if you've considered applying with other ideas, list them here," I wrote that if I had significant capital I would build an AI system using GPUs to create a language model, because AI would need to master language first.

Still nothing.

So here I am — pushing out ejection predictions, ISW anomaly confirmations, and computational scaling results from a laptop in Kauai, trying to make rent on time consistently, get my foot in the door for some financial stability, and maybe start a family.

This is the tip of the iceberg on this story. Any dollars are appreciated. Maybe when I solve for GUT it'll be enough.
